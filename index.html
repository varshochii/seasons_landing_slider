Autumn Season

Autumn, also known as fall in North American English, is one of the four temperate seasons. Outside the tropics, autumns marks the transition from summer to winter, in September or Murch. Autumn is the season when the duration of daylight becomes noticeably shorter and the temperature cools considerably.

Winter Season

Winter is the coldest season of the year in polar and temperate zones. It occurs between autumn and spring. The title of the Earth's axis causes seasons. Winter occurs when a hemisphere is oriented away from the sun. Different Cultures define different dates as the start of winter, and some use a definition based on weather
